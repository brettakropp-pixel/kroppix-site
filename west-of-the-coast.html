West of the Coast

A shift inland from the edge of the water into dry country, distance, and slower time.

This set moves between wide land, fading light, small encounters, and time spent working the ground.

Charters Towers Julia Creek Richmond Cloncurry Clem Walton Ballara

Distance

The land opens quickly. Low hills, dry grass, and long distances where very little interrupts the horizon.

Light

Light becomes the main change. Heat softens into colour, and the day resolves itself across sky and grass.

Encounters

Small encounters break the rhythm. Brief, quiet moments that sit inside the larger stillness.

Ground

Time shifts again here. Slower, more deliberate. Working the ground, searching, turning over small fragments that carry a much longer history.

Traces

Signs of presence remain, but lightly. Structures, tracks, and places that sit between use and memory.

West of the Coast — western Queensland.
